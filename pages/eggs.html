Real Scrambled Eggs

It was at the beginning of COVID where I watched Gordon Ramsay on YouTube tell me that I have been making scrambled eggs the wrong way. What I had been making was really just a chopped up omelette. Ramsay went on to make this soft scramble and I just had to try it. I haven't and won't got back. Try it out. If you think they don't look done, it's okay that's how they are suppose to look, they are done and they are delicious. This is just a positive take-away from the COVID era in my life.

Ingredients

  • 1 "Knob" of Butter (1 Tbsp.)
  • 3 Eggs
  • 1 tsp. Crême Fraîche (or Sour Cream)
  • 1 Tbsp. Fresh Chopped Chives
  • Salt and Pepper to Taste

Steps

  1. Eggs and butter in a saucepan at medium heat.
  2. Constantly stir while pan is on heat for 30 seconds then stir off heat for 30 seconds. Repeat this for 2 or so.
  3. After cooking the eggs remove from heat and add the Crême Fraîche or Sour Creme to stop the cooking.
  4. Lightly fold in the chives and add salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Plate on a peice of toast.
  6. Boom, these are real scrambled eggs. Let go of those omelettes.
